Insights & Final Report

📈 Fidelity Asia — Best Risk-Adjusted

Annualized return 15.69%, Sharpe ratio 0.723 — best among all three. Moderate volatility (21.68%) makes it the recommended core holding.

⚡ Invesco — High Return, High Risk

Highest return (23.17%) but extreme volatility (43.15%). Lowest Sharpe (0.537). The max daily return of 164.53% suggests a data anomaly requiring investigation.

🛡 Matthews Asia — Most Stable

Lowest std deviation (0.01213) and fewest extreme outliers. Best suited for capital preservation investors seeking controlled Asian equity exposure.

🔗 Fidelity–Matthews Over-Correlated

Correlation of 0.834 means holding both provides minimal diversification. Invesco's lower correlation (~0.42) is the better diversifier.

🧮 PCA — One Factor Dominates

PC1 explains 71.53% of total variance — a dominant Asian market beta. Just 2 PCs capture 94.46%, confirming 2D reduction without significant loss.

⚠ 300+ Outliers Per Fund

IQR method flags 305–328 extreme days per fund — corresponding to GFC (2008), COVID-19 (Mar 2020), and 2022 rate-hike cycle.

📊 ANOVA — Means Are Equal

One-way ANOVA (F=0.87, p=0.42): no significant mean difference. Fund selection must be driven by risk tolerance, not expected return.

🎯 Bayes — Strong Market Amplifier

On market-up days, P(fund gain) rises to ~90% — a Bayesian lift of 67–71%. All three act as strong beta amplifiers of Asian sentiment.
